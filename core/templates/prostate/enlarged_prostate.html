What You Might Want to Know About Enlarged Prostate

A Quick Side View

Placed in-between the bladder and penis, the prostate is an important part of the male reproduction system. It sorrounds the urethra and secretes the fluid that nourishes and protects the semen. Prostate increases in size as men get older. However, its over-enlargement, overtime can lead to symptoms, other complications and health conditions that will need to be treated.

BPH

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH), simply refers to enlargement of the prostate gland in men as a result of multiplication of cells. The additional cells makes the prostate gland to increase, which clamps down on the urethra, blocking the flow urine, and can affect the quality of life significantly.

What Causes BPH?

BPH is a normal situation as male ages, even though the exact cause is not known, it's beliveved that changes in males sex hormones which accompanies aging can be behind it.

Symptoms of BPH

Initially, the symptoms will not be severe, but things can get blown up if it's left untreated. Typical signs of BPH will include the following:

  • Slowed or delayed urination
  • Blood in urine
  • Painful urination
  • Weak urinary stream
  • Urinating more than two or three times at nights
  • Sudden urge to urinate
  • Leakages of urine
  • Inability to empty bladder completely

Prostate Diagnosis

Prostate tests usually start with physical examination by the doctor and by obtaining medical history. Other tests will include:

  • Prostate-specific antigen (PSA): A blood test to check prostate cancer.
  • Post-void residual: Used to determine the amount of urine that remains in the bladder.
  • Urinalysis: Checking of urine for blood and bacteria
  • Urology: X-Ray or TC scan done by injecting dye into your body
